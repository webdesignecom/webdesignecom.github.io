Can Ecommerce Be Used for Services?

Yes, Ecommerce can be used to sell services in addition to physical and digital products. Businesses offering services like consulting, booking appointments, rentals, or custom work can use Ecommerce to showcase their offerings, manage payments, and schedule appointments. With the help of apps and customizations, businesses can create service-specific features such as booking forms, subscription billing, and personalized service options.

Does Ecommerce Sell Services?

Ecommerce itself doesn’t sell services directly; instead, it provides a platform for businesses to sell their products or services. Through Ecommerce, service-based businesses can create and manage online stores, enabling them to list services, accept payments, and engage with clients. Ecommerce also offers apps and tools that make it easier for service providers to customize their stores to suit their needs.

Does Ecommerce Have a Staging Site?

Ecommerce does not natively offer a dedicated staging site, but it does provide workarounds. Store owners can create a duplicate of their theme and make changes in the unpublished version, which serves as a staging environment. For more advanced needs, third-party apps like Rewind Staging allow merchants to create a separate staging site for testing without affecting the live store.
